Tending Our Earth With Tender Hands

              
She turns slowly beneath the stars,
Draped in oceans and memory.
A cradle of breath, of root and rain,
Where the sky hums lullabies to the land.


Her bones are stone, her veins molten gold—
Ancient and pulsing beneath our step.
She wears seasons like shawls of time,
Spinning tales in winds and blossoming echoes.



Earth does not speak loudly.
She sings through rivers,
Weeps through forests felled,
And smiles in petals that rise again.

Flowing Through Time

I am not finished.I change every day.My mountains rise and fall. My rivers shift.My climate warms.I am shaped by the stars,by time and by you.
